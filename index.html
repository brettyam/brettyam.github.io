MediSync

Web Development

Video Credit: Nina Dang
  • Project Type: Academic
  • Date: January - June 2015
  • Role: Developer

MediSync is a senior Capstone project by fellow students David Gutierrez, Nina Dang, Troy Griffiths and myself. Its intention is to provide a safer way for patients to schedule their medication consumption by alerting users when they are due for a dosage intake. Additionally, the app's main value is that it's capable of cross referencing a user's current medication schedule against itself when adding drugs and warns the user if a harmful drug interaction is detected. It would ideally account for each type of drug in the schedule, the dosage amount, and the time relative to every other dosage intake when considering the severity of a potential interaction.

The result of the project is intended to be a proof of concept since the accuracy of the results are very sensitive to a rich and robust drug database that would be best created by qualified doctors and pharmacists. However, it does accurately represent the desired interaction that a user would experience when using the application, along with demonstrating our web development dexterity at the time in AngularJS, Node.js, MongoDB, and Express.

San Francisco Yellow Cab

Mobile UX

  • Project Type: Academic
  • Date: Jan - Mar 2014
  • Role: UX Designer

This project was assigned in an information architecture course. We worked in groups of two with the goal of creating a complete user experience in which a user would like to schedule a taxi ride from point A to B but would like to share the ride with other individuals with a similar path in order to split the cost. Doing so required a strong understanding of the problem space, the user scenarios, creating respective flow charts, and taking account for unexpected edge cases. We also got some exposure to designing with branding strategies in mind.

The end result of the project was an interactive prototype in Axure that reflected the user flow we had arrived at based on our research. Aesthetically it is not meant to represent what we could expect a final version to look like, but it is what we could expect the interaction to feel like. However, above are a couple of higher fidelity mockups that we built towards the end of the project.

Encaption Mobile App

Mobile UX & Research

  • Project Type: Academic/Client - Laura Ackerman
  • Date: Sept - Dec 2013
  • Role: UX Designer & Project Manager

This is one of my more favorite projects. It was unique in that we worked with a disabled (half blind and half deaf) user who would be our subject matter expert in designing a voice-to-text translator application. This required us to delve into much user research including interviews and usability testing with her every two weeks in which the design was being challeneged by the user, not the user being challenged by the design. Deliverables also ranged from creating paper prototypes, a higher fidelity prototype in Balsamiq, and a design specification which can be found by viewing the full project. The end of the project essentially mimics the design spec being passed on to a team of developers for building. Disclaimer: it is quite extensive!

The project was also very humbling to be a part of. Not only did our SME improve our design, but she also reminded us of how lucky we are as human beings to have full capability of our senses that we often take for granted. A more personalized reflection of the process can also be found here.

Bubble Mobile OS

Mobile UX & Design

  • Project Type: Academic
  • Date: Apr - June 2014
  • Role: UX and Visual Designer

For this project, the class as a single team was assigned with the task of designing a brand new mobile operating system in just ten weeks. The idea the class decided on was "Bubble", an OS that placed its value on multitasking and speed without a centralized home screen.

For the first eight weeks we worked on developing a new, unique design language and branding strategies to make the overall experience feel as unified and consistent as possible. A nice styleguide was also created by fellow students that the class was meant to adhere to. The final two weeks required us to break off into subteams, responsible for designing the UX and respective UI's for simple use cases within expected first party OS apps. Our team was accountable for the Music Player application and designing the user experience around simply finding and playing a desired song.
